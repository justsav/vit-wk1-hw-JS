Welcome to RAGBRAI: The Register's Annual Great Bike Ride Across Iowa

Every year, thousands of cyclists descend on to the back highways and biways of the Hawkeye State to traverse the width of the state of Iowa, from East to West over 7 days, on bicycles. The annual event began in 1973 and has only increased in popularity with every passing year, and a route that changes every year to stop overnight in a new set of towns.

In 44 years, RAGBRAI has passed through 780 Iowa towns, spent the night in 125 different overnight towns. In 2013, an estimated 36,000 riders were counted on the road from Perry to Des Moines.

In 2022, RAGBRAI will run from July 23-30, start in Sergeant Bluff and end in Lansing. Riders will overnight in Ida Grove, Pocahontas, Emmetsburg, Mason City, Charles City and West Union.

The northern route this year is around 430 miles and nearly 11,900 feet of climb, making it the eleventh shortest ride and eighteenth flattest.
